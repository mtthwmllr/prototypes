AI Content Governance Framework
A practical guide for responsible AI-assisted content production for a company with fidicuary accountability. Not a policy document — a working tool.

    Risk tiers at a glance
    Tier 1 — Low risk AI can assist freely
    Internal drafts Research summaries Briefing prep Concept notes
    Author reviews before sharing. No formal sign-off required.
    Accuracy spot-check encouraged. Brand voice applies.
    Tier 2 — Medium risk Human review required
    Social (corporate) Client Portal Intranet Investment education Event comms
    Reviewed by content lead for accuracy, brand voice, and source verification before publication.
    All factual claims traced to primary sources. No AI-generated citations accepted without verification.
    Manager sign-off required if content touches investment performance or ESG positioning.
    Tier 3 — High risk Senior authority required
    Annual Report Stewardship Report Client communications Media statements Regulatory submissions
    AI may assist with drafting only. No AI-generated content published without full human authorship review.
    Legal and/or Compliance review required prior to publication. Investment accuracy verified by subject matter authority.
    VP, Communications sign-off required. Disclosure standards documented and on file.
    Governing principles
    1
    AI assists, humans decide. No AI output is published into a high-stakes context without qualified human review. The goal is to extend judgement, not replace it.
    2
    Risk scales with audience and consequence. Internal use carries low risk. Client-facing and compliance-adjacent content carries high risk. Classification determines the review path, not the tool used.
    3
    Sources must be traceable. All factual claims in Tier 2 and Tier 3 content must trace back to a verified primary source. AI-generated citations are not accepted without independent verification.
    4
    Governance enables adoption. This framework is designed to give the team confidence to use AI tools — not to restrict them. Clear rules remove hesitation and protect the organization from preventable risk.
    5
    The framework evolves. AI tools and regulatory expectations are moving quickly. This framework is reviewed quarterly and updated as the company's AI adoption matures.
